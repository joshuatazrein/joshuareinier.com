Joshua Reinier presents Hauntings, a Multimedia Installation

Oberlin senior Joshua Reinier presents a ghostly experience involving dancers, spoken word, and immersive visuals.

Funded by an Oberlin XARTS Grant, the piece combines elements of spoken word and sound design to present a fragmented narrative that emerges fluidly from the circulating performers. Audience members are invited to walk freely through the hall as performers repeat text fragments resembling passing conversation along with a swirling sonic tapestry. Winds blow, images coalesce and dissolve on surrounding projectors. The piece both immerses and defies, swirling as it began.

Sunday, November 11 at 7:30 PM, Wilder Main

Joshua Reinier

Reinier is a vocalist, composer, and multi-instrumentalist whose searching compositions vibrate at the edge of language. His work surveys a range of styles, from poetry and electronic music to satirical theatre-pieces incorporating graphic scores and improvisation. Drawing inspiration from visionaries such as Peter Maxwell Davies and George Lewis, Reinier advances an openminded approach which sits comfortably in the expanding contemporary scene. Listeners may be reminded of performer/composers like Jennifer Walshe and Wet Ink.
